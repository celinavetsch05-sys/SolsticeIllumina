The Brief

Verena Larcher and Matthias Fahrion run a personal vacation rental business in Oberammergau, Bavaria. Their philosophy: "We don't just rent apartments — we want you to feel truly at home, like with friends." They needed a logo, a brand system, and a full website that carried that warmth from the first click — calm, honest, and inviting, not a hotel brochure. I delivered both the brand identity and a responsive website with interactive features and SEO built in from the start.

Features Built

Responsive Design
Optimised for Desktop (3 columns), Tablet (2 columns), and Mobile (1 column) — large tap targets and clear buttons on every device.
Interactive Elements
Gentle scroll fade-in animations, hover effects on apartment cards, and a filter function for browsing apartments by group size.
Sticky Navigation
Branded sticky nav in the primary blue with logo, page links, and a prominent booking CTA — visible at every scroll position.
SEO & Performance
Semantic HTML structure, fast load times, and correct meta data — so Verena and Matthias can be found on Google from day one.

What I Learned

The biggest challenge wasn't the code — it was the tone. Hospitality websites fail when they sound like brochures. Every headline and layout decision on this project was filtered through one question: does this feel like a friend recommending a place to stay? Getting that right, while also building a site that's technically solid and SEO-ready, was the most valuable thing I took away from this project.
